Let's learn about finding density! To find density, we have to divide an object's mass and volume.
But to find the mass and volume, we have to use a triple beam balance and a graduated cylinder.

Here is an example:

Explanation

Let's take the blue cube in the picture for example, when the blue cube
is placed on the triple beam balalnce, there's 3 little arrows that points on
3 numbers. Add those 3 numbers and you get the mass of the blue cube.
Another name for mass is g(grams). When we place the blue cube in the graduated cylinder,
the water rises and the water limit is on the number 68 and you get the volume.
Another name for volume is cm3. Also the 3 numbers on the
triple beam balance add up to 148.5. Now to find the density of the blue cube,
we divide 148.5 by 68, and that equals 2.18 g/cm3.
